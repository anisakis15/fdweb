UX/UI DESIGN

BIKERS Website

For my project "BIKERS", I designed a prototype of the interface of a small online bicycle store using Figma. I chose a color palette of red, black, and white, to evoke energy and action as well as contrast and clarity. These colors not only emphasize CTAs and buttons but also contribute to an overall clean and user-friendly design. The layout includes fixed elements such as the help bar, header, and navigation bar for easier access across the site. Icons for the cart, user profile, and search are intuitive, guiding users smoothly through the shopping process.

Typography was chosen with readability and hierarchy in mind: "LEMON MILK" for bold titles and CTAs, and Louis George Café for body text and navigation, ensuring clarity and legibility. Buttons are clearly labeled, center-aligned, and visually prominent to encourage action. The imagery, which was AI-generated with my own prompts to ensure stylistic consistency, depicts bikes and accessories in motion, helping users visualize the products clearly. The shopping cart overlay guides the user toward completing the purchase with a prominent CTA, reinforcing the purchase flow with thoughtful visual hierarchy.

ZONE OUT Mobile App

Another prototype I created using Figma is ZONE OUT, a mobile app focused on providing a calm, intuitive, and supportive experience for users dealing with stress or anxiety. The app combines relaxation tools, like soothing videos, ASMR, and meditation content, with practical resources such as emergency contacts and support groups. A clean layout and smooth navigation help users access what they need quickly and comfortably, whether it’s to relax or seek help.

For the visual design, I used a calming blue palette paired with white for clarity, while each content category is color-coded (turquoise for "Meditate," orange for "Funny") to reinforce emotional tone. I used soft gradients, AI-generated imagery, and a mix of serif and sans-serif typography to balance aesthetics with readability. Key features include large CTAs for cases of emergency, community support access, and a breathing animation during emergency calls to help users stay grounded.
