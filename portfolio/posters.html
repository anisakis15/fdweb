POSTERS AND CAMPAIGNS

STOP THE BAN Campaign

"Stop the Ban" is a graphic campaign for a class assignment that criticizes the growing censorship in the U.S. affecting freedom of expression in art, literature, and education.

Using bold posters with clear visual metaphors, which represent this censor through pixels and black marker, highlights the impact of laws like “Don’t Say Gay” and book bans on critical topics like race and gender.

Through persuasive design elements, including color, scale, and Gestalt principles, the campaign raises awareness about cultural repression and defends the right to inclusive, unrestricted education.

GALLO STUDIOS Design

For the Gallo Studios project, I designed a triptych poster that captures the essence of the studio's creative vision. The design incorporates bold typography, vibrant colors, and abstract shapes to convey a sense of innovation and artistic expression.

The triptych format allows for a dynamic storytelling approach, with each panel representing a different aspect of the studio's work. The left panel focuses on the studio's history, the center panel highlights its current projects, and the right panel envisions its future endeavors.

This project challenged me to think critically about composition, color theory, and visual hierarchy, resulting in a cohesive and impactful design that resonates with the studio's audience.
